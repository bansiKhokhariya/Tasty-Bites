Paneer Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala, also known as Butter Paneer, is one of the most popular vegetarian curries in India. Found on restaurant menus across the country, this dish has earned its place as a true North Indian classic. The recipe is loved for its silky tomato-based gravy, rich buttery taste, and the tender chunks of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) that absorb all the flavor. It’s indulgent, comforting, and perfect for pairing with naan, roti, paratha, or even steamed basmati rice. Whether you’re making it for a family dinner, festive spread, or a special occasion, Paneer Butter Masala never disappoints.

At the heart of Paneer Butter Masala is its base gravy—a blend of tomatoes, onions, cashews, and aromatic spices cooked slowly until smooth and rich. The cashews give body and creaminess without making the dish overly heavy, while the tomatoes add a subtle tanginess that balances the richness of butter and cream. The dish gets its signature restaurant-style flavor from slow-cooked spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala, along with whole spices such as cardamom, cinnamon, and bay leaf. Together, they build layers of flavor that make every bite deeply satisfying.

The paneer itself plays a starring role. Fresh, soft paneer cubes are gently simmered in the prepared gravy so they remain tender while soaking in the rich, spiced sauce. Some chefs lightly pan-fry the paneer for a firmer texture and golden crust, while others keep it soft and delicate. Both approaches work, depending on whether you prefer a slight bite or melt-in-the-mouth softness. A generous knob of butter stirred in at the end, along with a swirl of fresh cream, brings the whole dish together with a luxurious finish.

Making Paneer Butter Masala at home is simpler than it looks. First, you sauté onions, ginger, garlic, and tomatoes until they break down into a thick, flavorful base. Cashews or almonds are blended in to give it that trademark restaurant-style silkiness. This mixture is then pureed into a smooth paste before being simmered again with butter, cream, and powdered spices. The trick is in cooking the masala patiently—low and slow, until oil begins to release from the sides. That’s when you know the flavors have truly developed. Once the paneer cubes are folded in, the dish is simmered briefly to bring everything together.

Like many Indian curries, Paneer Butter Masala has variations. Some versions lean sweeter, with honey or sugar balancing the spices, while others turn up the heat with extra chili powder. Restaurants often use food color for a bright orange-red hue, but at home you can achieve the same naturally with Kashmiri red chili powder and ripe tomatoes. A vegan version can be made with tofu instead of paneer and coconut cream in place of dairy cream, keeping the richness intact while making it plant-based.

Pairing is key to enjoying Paneer Butter Masala fully. It’s most often eaten with butter naan, garlic naan, or soft tandoori roti, as the slightly chewy bread soaks up the creamy gravy beautifully. Steamed jeera rice or vegetable pulao is another excellent pairing. For a complete North Indian meal, it can be served alongside dal makhani, jeera rice, and a fresh salad. A sprinkle of fresh coriander on top not only adds color but also cuts through the richness.

Storage is easy too. Paneer Butter Masala can be refrigerated in an airtight container for 2–3 days. The gravy thickens upon cooling, so when reheating, add a splash of water or milk to bring it back to its original consistency. Freezing is possible as well—store the gravy base without paneer, then add fresh paneer cubes after reheating to maintain their texture.

Nutritionally, Paneer Butter Masala is rich in protein (from paneer) and calcium, though it’s also high in fat due to butter and cream. For a lighter version, you can cut down on butter, replace cream with milk, and use homemade paneer made from toned milk. Despite its indulgent nature, it can be enjoyed in moderation as part of a balanced diet, and it remains one of the most rewarding vegetarian comfort foods.

What makes Paneer Butter Masala universally appealing is its balance—creamy yet tangy, spicy yet mildly sweet, luxurious yet simple to make. It carries the warmth of traditional Indian spices but presents them in a way that’s approachable even to those new to Indian cuisine. Whether served in a fine-dining restaurant or made in a humble kitchen, this dish continues to symbolize Indian comfort food at its best.

Ingredients

  • 250g paneer, cubed
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare tempering: Heat butter in a pan, add cumin seeds and ginger-garlic paste, sauté till aromatic.
  2. Add tomato puree: Pour in tomato puree, cook for 5-7 minutes until oil separates.
  3. Spices: Mix in turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook for 2 minutes.
  4. Add paneer & cream: Gently fold in paneer cubes and cream, simmer for 5 minutes on low heat.
  5. Garnish & serve: Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with roti or naan.

Tips for Perfect Paneer Butter Masala

Soft Paneer

Use fresh, soft paneer for creamy texture and better taste.

Balanced Spice

Adjust chili powder and garam masala according to your spice preference.

Serve Hot

Serve immediately to enjoy the rich, buttery flavor of the curry.
