Delicious Samosa Recipe

Samosa is one of the most loved Indian snacks, enjoyed not just in India but all over the world. This golden, crispy, and triangular snack has a flaky crust on the outside and a deliciously spiced potato filling on the inside. Traditionally deep-fried, samosas are served hot with tangy chutneys like mint chutney or sweet tamarind chutney, making them the perfect tea-time companion.

The origins of samosa can be traced back to the Middle East, where it was known as “sambosa.” Over time, it traveled to India through traders and became the iconic street food we know today. In India, samosa has evolved into a cultural staple. From the streets of Delhi to the food stalls of Mumbai, every region has its own version of this snack. Some use peas and potatoes as the main filling, while others experiment with paneer, lentils, chicken, or even minced lamb.

Making samosas at home is not just about cooking—it is about reliving memories. Almost every Indian household has a story attached to samosas. Whether it is Sunday family gatherings, college canteen memories, or roadside stalls, samosa has always been a part of our daily lives. Its crunchy bite and spicy filling bring comfort and joy like no other snack.

The secret to a good samosa lies in the dough and the frying technique. The outer crust should be stiff yet flaky, while the filling should be aromatic and well-seasoned. Common spices include cumin, coriander powder, garam masala, turmeric, and green chilies. To ensure the perfect crispiness, samosas should always be fried on medium flame so the crust cooks evenly without absorbing too much oil.

Today, samosa is not just an Indian dish—it has gone global. In countries like the UK, USA, and Canada, Indian restaurants proudly serve samosas as appetizers. In East Africa, you will find meat-filled samosas sold on every corner. In Central Asia, baked samosas are preferred over fried ones. This global popularity makes samosa one of the few snacks that have crossed cultural and geographical boundaries with ease.

If you are health-conscious, you can even try baked or air-fried samosas. While the classic fried version is unbeatable in taste, the healthier alternatives still manage to keep the flavor intact. You can also get creative by experimenting with fillings like cheese, sweet corn, mushrooms, or even chocolate for a dessert twist.

In India, samosa is often paired with masala chai, making it the ultimate monsoon treat. Imagine sitting by the window, enjoying the rain, sipping hot tea, and biting into a crispy samosa—it is pure happiness. No wonder samosa is called the “king of Indian street food.”

Whether you are making it for a family party, festival, or evening snack, this homemade samosa recipe will never disappoint. With simple ingredients and easy steps, you can prepare the same crispy, golden, and mouth-watering samosas at home that you usually enjoy from street vendors.

In this recipe guide, you will learn how to prepare the dough, make the perfect filling, and fold the samosas into their signature triangular shape. We have also included tips and tricks to ensure your samosas turn out crunchy, flavorful, and absolutely delicious.

Once you try this recipe, you will realize that homemade samosas taste even better than the store-bought ones. So let’s dive into the ingredients and start preparing this timeless Indian snack that has won hearts across the world. Trust us, with a little patience and practice, you will master the art of making crispy, golden samosas at home!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Oil for deep frying
  • 2-3 medium potatoes, boiled & mashed
  • 1/2 cup peas, boiled
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare dough: Mix flour, salt, oil, and carom seeds with water to form a stiff dough. Set aside.
  2. Make filling: Sauté cumin, ginger, chilies, potatoes, peas, coriander powder, and salt.
  3. Shape samosas: Divide dough, roll into ovals, cut, fill, and seal edges.
  4. Fry samosas: Heat oil and fry until golden brown & crispy.
  5. Serve hot: Garnish with fresh coriander & serve with chutney or ketchup.

Tips for Perfect Samosas

Crispy Samosas

Use stiff dough and fry on medium heat to ensure crispiness without burning.

Flavorful Filling

Add roasted spices, green chilies, and coriander for a rich taste.

Perfect Shape

Seal edges properly to prevent filling from leaking while frying.
