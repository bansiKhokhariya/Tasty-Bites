Delicious Poha Recipe

Poha, also known as flattened rice, is one of the simplest yet most satisfying Indian breakfast dishes. Light, fluffy, and nutritious, it is prepared in countless households across the country every morning. Whether you’re rushing for office, preparing tiffin for kids, or craving a wholesome yet quick snack, poha always comes to the rescue.

At its core, poha is made from rice that has been parboiled, flattened, and dried into light flakes. These flakes swell up when rinsed, turning soft yet retaining their shape, which makes them ideal for cooking. The dish usually combines poha with onions, green chilies, curry leaves, turmeric, roasted peanuts, and a squeeze of lemon juice.

The beauty of poha lies in its adaptability. In Maharashtra, you’ll find the famous kanda poha (onion poha), often served with sev. Indore takes it a step further with Indori poha, which is slightly sweet and topped with spicy sev, pomegranate seeds, and sometimes even jalebi on the side. Down south, versions include adding coconut and tempered spices. Gujarat adds a touch of sweetness. This ability to blend with regional tastes makes poha a dish for everyone.

Apart from being tasty, poha is also healthy. Since it is made from parboiled rice, it retains nutrients and is easier to digest compared to plain rice. It is gluten-free, light on the stomach, and provides quick energy, which is why it’s often recommended for breakfast.

Making poha at home is quick and easy. The key is rinsing the poha gently under running water and letting it sit for a few minutes to absorb moisture without becoming mushy. The tempering of mustard seeds, curry leaves, and green chilies forms the flavor base. Onions add sweetness, while turmeric gives it the signature yellow hue.

Beyond breakfast, poha also serves as a reliable snack or even a light dinner. It is often served with chai during rainy evenings or paired with buttermilk in summers for a refreshing twist. Travelers and hostellers love poha because it is inexpensive, filling, and easy to prepare in large batches.

Poha also has cultural importance. In central India, it is the go-to dish for welcoming guests in the morning. Weddings and special occasions often feature poha in the breakfast spread. In Indore, a city famous for its street food, poha-jalebi is not just a dish but an identity.

Another charm of poha is how customizable it is. If you like spice, add extra chilies or sev. If you enjoy sweetness, a dash of sugar works wonders. For a healthier version, add sprouts, grated carrots, or paneer. For indulgence, serve with jalebi or namkeen.

To sum it up, poha is more than just a breakfast recipe—it is a part of everyday life in India. It symbolizes simplicity, health, and tradition, while being adaptable enough to please modern tastes.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup flattened rice (poha)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp roasted peanuts
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Rinse poha: Wash poha in a colander under running water, drain well, and set aside.
  2. Prepare tempering: Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, curry leaves, and chopped green chilies.
  3. Sauté onions: Add finely chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  4. Add turmeric & poha: Mix in turmeric powder, salt, and then gently fold in the drained poha.
  5. Finish & garnish: Add sugar (if using), roasted peanuts, lemon juice, and garnish with fresh coriander. Serve hot.

Tips for Perfect Poha

Soft Poha

Soak poha lightly, don’t over-soak, to retain softness without becoming mushy.

Flavor Boost

Add roasted peanuts and a dash of lemon juice for extra crunch and tanginess.

Quick Breakfast

This dish takes only 15 minutes, perfect for busy mornings.
